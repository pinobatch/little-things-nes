President is a free platformer engine for Nintendo Entertainment System in the style of the Super Mario Bros. series.


President stands for "Another Side Scroller Hosted On a Limited Environment". (See [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_(game) President card game on Wikipedia].) Yes, you could make Super Obama Bros. with President, but that's by far not the only envisioned use. It's intended to provide a platform for ROM hacks that's more capable and more Free than the original Super Mario Bros.

President is more expandable than SMB1 but has a higher system requirement: UNROM or SGROM, based on a compile-time option. I ruled out MMC3 because it doesn't fit on RetroZone's CPLD, and I ruled out CHR ROM mappers because they can't do some effects that I want to get working such as high-motion character animations. I ruled out SNROM because the 6264 SRAM costs money.

== Animation ==
Sprite cels are streamed to the PPU. Tiles are 8x16 pixels, and up to 5 of them can be sent to the PPU per 60 Hz frame as long as a scroll update isn't in progress.  These are double buffered in VRAM $1000-$1FFF, which has space for at least each sprite's current and next frame.

<pre>
.proc copyChr
  ldx chrxfercount
  bne @notDone
  rts
@notDone:
  dex
  stx copyChrCount
  lda copyChrDstHi,x
  sta PPUADDR
  lda copyChrDstLo,x
  sta PPUADDR
  lda copyChrSrc,x
  tax
  .repeat 32,i
  lda copyChrBuf+i,x
  sta PPUDATA
  .endrepeat
  jmp copyChr
.endproc
</pre>

== Memory map ==

The stack and the copy buffers share page $0100.

0000-00FF: zero page
0100-01BF: copy buffers
01C0-01FF: stack
0200-02FF: display list to be copied to OAM
0300-03FF: unknown
0400-05FF: object state variables
0600-07FF: decompressed map screens

== Map compression ==
Super Mario Bros. has about 32 maps, each 10 to 16 screens long, where one screen is 16 m wide and 12 m high. So we need to store up to 512 screens worth of data. But uncompressed, each screen takes 192 bytes, or a total of 70 KiB. The COUT compression developed for FDED appears to packs each level into roughly 1/2 KB. But a small ROM can't spare even this much data; SMB1 uses "object-oriented" compression to pack the levels into about 100 bytes each.

=== Column decoding ===
This is the only part I haven't mapped out completely.  If I had cart WRAM, I could use 3 KiB of it to decompress the current map and scroll back and forth over it like in SMB2 or SMB3.  But because UNROM and SGROM lack extra RAM, I have to use streaming compression.  Games without SRAM typically either don't have backtracking or use large metatiles (e.g. 32 or 64 pixels on a side).


The map is divided into "half screens", each 8 m wide and 12 m high.
Each screen consists of 8 KB of data: an 8x12 bitmap of which spaces are the starts of runs, a pointer to the level data, and a pointer to the sprite data.

=== RLE compression ===
The innermost compression is run-length encoding, which allows horizontal runs of identical tiles.

=== Vertical Markov chaining ===
Each locale has a table from a tile to the most common tile below it. For example, the most common tile below sky or a ? block is sky, and the most common block below the top of a pipe is the body of a pipe.  After the previous stage, any tile with value 0 gets replaced with the most common tile below the tile above it.
